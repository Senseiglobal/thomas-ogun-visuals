Overview

One clear idea, carried from concept to release.

OMO needed more than a music video. The project required a visual identity that could move naturally between Lagos and Cape Town, then remain consistent across broadcast, radio, press, streaming and social media.

I stayed hands-on throughout: developing the treatment, directing and shooting the film, guiding casting and production design, leading the edit and VFX, and helping shape the campaign's promotion and release.

I intentionally chose a filmic visual style that would feel timeless rather than trend-led. Natural texture, considered framing and a restrained colour palette give the work an evergreen quality that can remain relevant for years.

The Process

A connected visual process.

The work moved from planning and visual development to production, post and release. Each stage strengthened the same story.

  1. Planning

    Turning the idea into a practical plan.

    A shared production board brought the treatment, references, schedule, equipment, locations and delivery dates together. It kept the Lagos and Cape Town teams aligned from the start.

  2. Lagos

    A visual language rooted in place.

    The Nigeria production moved between street life and controlled interiors. Through my leadership and creative art direction, these textures connected naturally with Cape Town's open landscapes. Each country kept its own character while contributing to one coherent visual story.

  3. Cape Town

    Expanding the world while keeping it connected.

    For Cape Town, I planned vehicle shots, drone movement and transitions around the changing light. Cool morning tones gradually gave way to warmer evening colour, creating a cinematic second chapter that still belonged to the same film.

  4. Production

    Directing performance, place and pace.

    I directed and shot the film across Lagos and Cape Town while also leading production design. Cast movement, styling, cars and landscape were composed to serve both the artist's story and the wider campaign.

  5. Post-Production

    Protecting the idea through the final cut.

    I edited the film and led its titles and VFX, carrying the visual rhythm into the finished piece. The final video became the centre of the broadcast, radio, press, playlist and social campaign.
